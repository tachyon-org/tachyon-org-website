Personnel

The Tachyon Resilient Modeling project brings together investigators, researchers, students, and external collaborators across institutions and national laboratories. The project is intended to combine real world datasets from operations of the Dept. of Energy's leadership computing facilities, with advanced modeling techniques to create predictive models that can be used to increase the resiliency and fault tolerance of scientific workflows.

The Tachyon project is lead by Rensellear Polytechnical Institute in collaboration with Fermi national Accelerator Laboratory and Argonne National Laboratory. We have collaborators from the University of California Davis as well as University Illinios, Chicago.

Dr. Kevin Brown

Co-Principal Investigator
Mathematics and Computer Science Division / Argonne National Lab

Dr. Kevin Brown is an Assistant Computer Scientist and the inaugural Walter Massey Fellow at Argonne, focusing on network performance analysis and simulation. His work includes accelerating parallel discrete event simulations with surrogate models and improving the resilience of geographically-distributed research infrastructure through multi-resolution modeling. Homepage · ORCID

Dr. Zhiling Lan

Co-Principal Investigator
Department of Computer Science / University of Illinois Chicago

Professor Zhiling Lan leads the SPEAR (Systems for Performance, Energy, and Resiliency) research group, with expertise in parallel and distributed systems and high-performance computing. Her work spans intelligent workload management, network simulation, energy efficiency, and AI-guided resource optimization for advanced cyberinfrastructure, and includes a joint appointment with Argonne National Laboratory.
